Band 7 model — two developed bodies

[FOR] Those who defend homework argue that it reinforces what is taught in class, and there is real force to this claim. A concept introduced in a single lesson is easily forgotten, whereas revisiting it at home helps to move it into long-term memory. Regular assignments also cultivate self-discipline, since children must learn to manage their time without a teacher standing over them. In many East Asian education systems, for instance, structured daily practice is widely credited with the strong results students achieve in mathematics — a habit of consistent effort that pays off over years, not weeks.

[AGAINST] Critics, however, contend that excessive homework can be counter-productive, and this concern deserves serious weight. Children who spend their evenings on repetitive worksheets have little time left for the play, rest, and family life that are equally vital to healthy development. Worse, homework can entrench inequality: a child with a quiet room and educated parents enjoys an advantage that a child in an overcrowded home simply does not. Rather than raising all students, then, poorly designed homework may quietly widen the gap between them.

FeatureWhy it's Band 7 development
One idea, gone deepEach paragraph takes a single claim (reinforcement / inequality) and explains the mechanism, rather than listing three thin points.
Specific example"East Asian systems … mathematics", "a quiet room and educated parents" — concrete, not "for example homework is good".
Evaluative language"there is real force to this claim", "deserves serious weight" — signals a thinking writer, lifting Task Response.
Lexis & grammarreinforces, cultivate self-discipline, entrench inequality, counter-productive; relative + concessive clauses used accurately.
Turkish L1 alert. ① "children" is already plural — no "childrens". ② "homework" is uncountable — never "homeworks" or "a homework"; say "a homework assignment". ③ Replace vague "very important" with the model's precise "equally vital / deserves serious weight". ④ Avoid "In addition to this situation" — use "Moreover" or "What is more".

Skill 2 Reading · True / False / Not Given (inference)

The hardest logic in Reading. For each answer decide: is it stated (True), contradicted (False), or simply absent (Not Given)? Quote the proof line.

The Case for Play

For decades, play was viewed by many educators as the opposite of learning — a pleasant distraction to be tolerated at break time but firmly separated from "real" schoolwork. Recent research in developmental psychology has overturned this view. Far from being trivial, unstructured play appears to be one of the most powerful ways in which young children learn.

When children build, invent games, or act out imaginary scenarios, they are in fact rehearsing complex skills. Negotiating the rules of a game teaches cooperation and compromise; constructing a tower and watching it fall teaches cause and effect more vividly than any lecture. Crucially, because the child chooses the activity, motivation is high and the lessons are absorbed almost without effort.

Some countries have responded by delaying formal instruction. In Finland, children do not begin formal schooling until the age of seven, spending their early years largely in play-based settings, and the country's students consistently perform well in international assessments. Advocates argue this is no coincidence, though critics point out that many other factors contribute to Finnish success.

What researchers agree on is that play should not be dismissed as mere entertainment. The challenge for schools is to protect time for it against mounting pressure to raise test scores — a pressure that, ironically, may be undermining the very foundations of learning it seeks to strengthen.

2FALSEReversed: play "teaches cause and effect more vividly than any lecture".
3NOT GIVENThe text says Finnish children start at seven, but never compares this to "most countries". No comparison = Not Given, not True.
4FALSE"play should not be dismissed as mere entertainment" — the opposite is stated.

Band 6 → 7 lesson: Q3 is the trap that catches most Band 6 students. The statement feels true and the passage mentions age seven — but the claim "later than most countries" is never made. Not Given = the passage doesn't say, even if it's probably true in real life. Answer from the text, not from your world knowledge.

Band 7 improvement focus

Depth in writing, discipline in reading. In the essay, if a paragraph has three shallow points, cut two and develop one — add the "because" and the "for example". In TFNG, never let real-world knowledge decide your answer; only the text can make something True or False. Absence of information is always Not Given.
