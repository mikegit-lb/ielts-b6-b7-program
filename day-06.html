Skill 1 Speaking · Part 2 long turn

Cue card

Describe a skill you learned that you have found useful.

You should say:
  • what the skill is
  • how and when you learned it
  • how long it took to become good at it
and explain why you have found this skill useful.
Set the ⏱ Timer. 1 minute planning — write bullet notes only (never full sentences), then talk for 2 minutes. Record it. Your notes should cover: what / how / how long / why useful.
Saved automatically.

Band 7 model (≈ 2 minutes spoken)

"The skill I'd like to talk about is touch-typing — being able to type without looking at the keyboard. It might sound a bit unglamorous, but it's genuinely one of the most useful things I've ever learned. I picked it up when I was about sixteen, mainly because I was embarrassingly slow at writing essays on the computer, so I found a free online programme and forced myself to practise fifteen minutes a day. Honestly, the first couple of weeks were frustrating — my speed actually dropped at first because I wasn't allowed to look down — but after roughly two months it clicked, and now I barely think about it. Why has it been so useful? Well, at university almost everything is done on a laptop, so being able to type quickly means I can keep up in lectures and get my ideas down before I forget them. It also saves an enormous amount of time when I'm working to a deadline. Looking back, it's funny that such a small, boring habit has ended up saving me hundreds of hours — I'd recommend it to anyone."
Technique: the speaker answers every bullet in order but never sounds like a list, because each point carries a small story or feeling ("embarrassingly slow", "it clicked"). The final "why useful" is developed with two reasons, not one. That's the Band 7 long turn: cover the card, but narrate it.

Skill 2 Writing Task 1 · bar chart (trend & comparison)

Your trend-verb toolkit (learn these 6)

rise / climb (go up) · surge / soar (go up fast) · fall / decline (go down) · plummet (go down fast) · level off (stop changing) · fluctuate (go up and down). Pair each with an adverb: rose steadily / sharply / dramatically.
Write at least 150 words. Overview first (which subjects grew, which fell, the biggest change), then group the data. Don't describe all 8 bars separately.
Saved automatically.

Band 7 model (≈ 175 words)

The bar chart compares the number of students enrolled in four evening-course subjects — languages, IT, art and business — in 2019 and 2023.

Overall, enrolment rose in three of the four subjects over the period, with IT seeing by far the largest increase, while art was the only subject to experience a decline.

IT enrolment more than doubled, surging from 90 students in 2019 to 200 in 2023, which made it the most popular subject by the end of the period. Languages also grew strongly, climbing from 120 to 180, while business rose more modestly from 60 to 110 — though this still represented a near doubling of its figure.

Art followed the opposite trend. Having been the best-attended subject in 2019, with 150 students, it slipped to 130 by 2023 and was overtaken by both IT and languages. As a result, the gap between the most and least popular subjects widened considerably over the four years.

CriterionWhy Band 7
Task achievementOverview names the pattern (3 rose, 1 fell, IT biggest) with no data; body then selects and compares key figures.
Lexical resourceVaried trend verbs: surging, climbing, rose modestly, slipped, was overtaken; "more than doubled", "near doubling".
CoherenceRisers grouped together; art contrasted ("the opposite trend"); a concluding comparison ("the gap … widened").
GrammarParticiple clause ("Having been the best-attended…"), passive ("was overtaken"), accurate comparatives.
Turkish L1 alert. ① "the number of students rose", not "was risen / was increased". ② "enrolment" is uncountable — no "enrolments" here. ③ Use "twice as many / more than doubled", not "two times more higher". ④ Don't start every sentence with the subject name — vary with "Having been…", "As a result…".

Band 7 improvement focus

Precision of verb = precision of meaning. "IT increased" is Band 6; "IT more than doubled, surging to 200" is Band 7 — it's accurate and lexically rich. Pick the verb that matches the exact movement (surge vs climb vs edge up). In speaking, the same precision applies: "it clicked", "my speed dropped" beat "it became good / bad".
