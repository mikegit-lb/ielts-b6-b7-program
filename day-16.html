Band 7 model (≈ 170 words)

The bar chart compares ownership of four technology devices between two age groups, 18–34 and 65+, in 2024.

Overall, younger adults reported higher ownership across most categories, particularly smartphones and smart speakers, whereas tablet ownership was the sole exception, with the older group slightly ahead.

Smartphone ownership showed the widest gap: 97% of 18–34-year-olds owned one, compared with 68% of those aged 65 and over. A similar pattern held for laptops, owned by 78% of younger adults against 61% of older ones, and smart speakers, where the younger group's ownership was roughly double that of the older group, at 38% versus 19%.

Tablets bucked this trend, being marginally more common among the 65+ group at 52%, against 45% for 18–34-year-olds — a narrow but notable reversal of the pattern seen elsewhere in the chart.

Audio script — Section 2

GUIDE: Good morning everyone, and welcome to the Tech Discovery Centre. Before we start the tour, a little bit of history — many visitors assume this building was purpose-built as a museum, but it was actually a printing factory until the 1990s, when it stood empty for over a decade before being converted. We officially opened our doors as the Tech Discovery Centre in 2015, following several years of renovation.

We now welcome around two hundred thousand visitors a year across four main zones. Last year, our Robotics Zone was easily the most visited area, overtaking the AI Interactive Zone, which had actually been our most popular attraction the year before that.

A quick note on entry — the main gate is used only for group bookings; individual visitors should use the side entrance instead, which is open every day including weekends.

Now, let me tell you where everything is. The Robotics Zone is on the ground floor, right past the entrance hall. The AI Interactive Zone is one floor up, on the first floor. If you're interested in gaming history, the Retro Gaming Room is on the second floor, right at the top of the building. And finally, our newest space, the Innovation Lab, is actually in the basement — it needs the extra soundproofing down there for some of the louder demonstrations.

Let's begin in the entrance hall.

Notice the pattern: every MCQ trap here relies on a time-shift word — "used to be", "the year before", "assume". This is the single most common Section 2 trap type, and it's genuinely exam-representative, not an invented difficulty.

Band 7 improvement focus

The exception is the story. In a Task 1 chart, the category that breaks the pattern is often what the examiner is really testing whether you'll notice — don't bury it. In listening, "used to be", "the year before", and "assumed" are your cue that the first thing you hear is not the answer.
