Retrieval 2 Strategy & trap recall

Answer from memory — this is the thinking that earns marks under pressure.

7In Listening, when a speaker says "…at first, but actually…", which value is usually the answer — the first or the second?
8In TFNG, if the passage simply doesn't mention the idea in the statement, the answer is _______.
9In Task 1, the overview sentence should / should not contain specific numbers?
10In Matching Headings, do you choose the heading by matching a repeated word, or by matching the paragraph's main idea?
#Answer
7The second value (the correction after "but actually…").
8Not Given (absence of information — never False).
9Should not — the overview names key features only; numbers go in the body.
10By the paragraph's main idea — a repeated word is often a distractor trap.

Band 7 improvement focus

Retrieval > re-reading. You just pulled a week of learning out of your own head — that effort of recall is what moves knowledge into permanent memory. Re-reading feels productive but fades fast. Every review day this program, you'll retrieve, not re-read. Trust it.
